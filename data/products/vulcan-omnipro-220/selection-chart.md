## RIGHT SIDE PANEL — The Duty Cycle

**Text (verbatim):**
> "The Duty Cycle is the number of minutes, within a 10-minute period, a welding process can produce the specified welding current without overheating."

### DUTY CYCLE EXAMPLE

**165A @ 30% Duty Cycle**

*(A clock/pie-chart diagram is shown illustrating the 10-minute period divided as follows:)*
- **5 Minutes Welding**
- **7 Minutes Resting**

*(Note: The clock graphic divides a 10-minute period showing the ON/OFF ratio for the duty cycle example at 165A, 30% duty cycle — meaning approximately 3 minutes welding and 7 minutes resting within every 10-minute period. The labels on the diagram read "5 Minutes Welding" and "7 Minutes Resting," though this appears inconsistent with a 30% duty cycle — transcribed verbatim as printed.)*
